title: "Chapter IV - Fundamental Rights"
description: "An updated presentation of Chapter IV of the Constitution of the Federal Republic of Nigeria (as amended) covering provisions on Fundamental Rights."
---




#### Section 33. Right to Life

1. Every person has a **right to life**, and no one shall be intentionally deprived of their life, except in the execution of a sentence by a court in respect of a criminal offence for which they have been found guilty in Nigeria.

2. A person shall not be regarded as having been deprived of life in contravention of this section if death results from the use of force, to such extent and in such circumstances as permitted by law, and reasonably necessary:
   - (a) **for the defence** of any person from unlawful violence or for the defence of property;
   - (b) **in order to effect a lawful arrest** or to prevent the escape of a person lawfully detained; or
   - (c) **for the purpose of suppressing a riot, insurrection, or mutiny.**

---

#### Section 34. Right to Dignity of Human Person

1. Every individual is entitled to **respect for the dignity of their person**, and accordingly:
   - (a) No person shall be subjected to **torture or inhuman or degrading treatment**;
   - (b) No person shall be held in **slavery or servitude**;
   - (c) No person shall be required to perform **forced or compulsory labour**.

2. For the purposes of subsection (1)(c), *forced or compulsory labour* excludes:
   - (a) Labour required in consequence of a court sentence or order;
   - (b) Labour required of members of the Armed Forces or Police Force in pursuance of their duties;
   - (c) Labour required of persons with conscientious objections to military service;
   - (d) Labour reasonably necessary in an emergency or calamity threatening the community;
   - (e) Labour forming part of:
     - (i) normal communal or civic obligations for community well-being;
     - (ii) compulsory national service in the Armed Forces as prescribed by law;
     - (iii) compulsory national service forming part of the education or training of Nigerian citizens as prescribed by law.

---

#### Section 35. Right to Personal Liberty

1. Every person is entitled to **personal liberty**, and no one shall be deprived of it except:
   - (a) In execution of a sentence for a criminal offence;
   - (b) For failure to comply with a court order;
   - (c) For the purpose of bringing a person before a court on reasonable suspicion of committing a crime;
   - (d) For the welfare or education of minors;
   - (e) For the care of persons with contagious diseases, mental illness, addiction, or vagrancy;
   - (f) To prevent unlawful entry or effect expulsion or extradition.

**Provided that**:  
A person awaiting trial shall not be detained longer than the maximum sentence prescribed for the offence.

2. Every arrested or detained person has the right to remain silent or avoid answering questions until after consulting a legal practitioner or a person of their choice.

3. Such a person must be informed, in writing and within 24 hours, in a language they understand, of the reasons for their arrest or detention.

4. A person detained under subsection (1)(c) must be brought before a court:
   - (a) Within **two months** if not entitled to bail;
   - (b) Within **three months** if released on bail.  
   Otherwise, they must be released unconditionally or on reasonable conditions.

5. “**A reasonable time**” is defined as:
   - (a) One day if a competent court is within 40 kilometres;
   - (b) Two days, or longer as deemed reasonable by the court, otherwise.

6. Any person unlawfully detained or arrested is entitled to **compensation and a public apology** from the appropriate authority.

7. This section does not:
   - (a) Apply to persons arrested for capital offences under subsection (4);
   - (b) Invalidate laws authorising detention for up to three months for members of the Armed Forces or Police under internal discipline.

---

#### Section 36. Right to Fair Hearing

1. In determining civil rights and obligations, including matters involving any government or authority, a person is entitled to a **fair hearing within a reasonable time** by an independent and impartial court or tribunal.

2. No law shall be invalid merely because it permits administrative authorities to determine civil rights and obligations, provided:
   - (a) Persons affected have an opportunity to make representations before decisions are made;
   - (b) The determination is not final and conclusive.

3. Court or tribunal proceedings, including decisions, shall be held in **public**.

4. Any person charged with a criminal offence is entitled to a **public hearing within a reasonable time**, unless:
   - (a) The court excludes the public for defence, safety, order, morality, minors' welfare, privacy protection, or justice;
   - (b) A Minister or State Commissioner establishes that public disclosure would harm public interest, prompting private evidence and protective measures.

5. A person charged with a criminal offence is **presumed innocent until proven guilty**.  
**Provided that**: Certain laws may impose the burden of proving particular facts on the accused without invalidating this presumption.

6. Every person charged with a criminal offence has the right to:
   - (a) Be informed promptly, in a language they understand, of the nature of the offence;
   - (b) Be given adequate time and facilities for defence preparation;
   - (c) Defend themselves personally or via a legal practitioner;
   - (d) Examine prosecution witnesses and obtain witnesses under the same conditions;
   - (e) Have, without payment, the assistance of an interpreter if unable to understand the language used at trial.

7. When tried, a person is entitled to a **record of proceedings** and copies of the judgment within seven days after the case concludes.

8. No one shall be guilty of a criminal offence for any act or omission that was not criminal at the time it occurred. No heavier penalty shall be imposed than what existed when the offence was committed.

9. A person tried and convicted or acquitted of an offence by a competent court cannot be retried for the same offence or one with the same ingredients, except on the order of a superior court.

10. A person pardoned for an offence shall not be retried for it.

11. No one tried for a criminal offence shall be **compelled to give evidence** at their trial.

12. No person shall be convicted of a criminal offence unless:
   - The offence is defined, and
   - The penalty prescribed in **a written law** enacted by the National Assembly, a State, or under their authority.


#### 37. Right to Private and Family Life

The privacy of citizens, their homes, correspondence, telephone conversations, and telegraphic communications is hereby guaranteed and protected.



#### 38. Right to Freedom of Thought, Conscience and Religion

1. Every person shall be entitled to freedom of thought, conscience, and religion, including freedom to change their religion or belief, and freedom (either alone or in community with others, and in public or in private) to manifest and propagate their religion or belief in worship, teaching, practice, and observance.

2. No person attending any place of education shall be required to receive religious instruction or to take part in or attend any religious ceremony or observance if such instruction, ceremony, or observance relates to a religion other than their own, or a religion not approved by their parent or guardian.

3. No religious community or denomination shall be prevented from providing religious instruction for pupils of that community or denomination in any place of education maintained wholly by that community or denomination.

4. Nothing in this section shall entitle any person to form, take part in the activity of, or be a member of a secret society.

---

#### 39. Right to Freedom of Expression and the Press

1. Every person shall be entitled to freedom of expression, including freedom to hold opinions and to receive and impart ideas and information without interference.

2. Without prejudice to the generality of subsection (1), every person shall be entitled to own, establish, and operate any medium for the dissemination of information, ideas, and opinions:

   > **Provided that** no person, other than the Government of the Federation or of a State or any other person or body authorised by the President on the fulfilment of conditions laid down by an Act of the National Assembly, shall own, establish, or operate a television or wireless broadcasting station for any purpose whatsoever.

3. Nothing in this section shall invalidate any law that is reasonably justifiable in a democratic society:
   - **(a)** for the purpose of preventing the disclosure of information received in confidence, maintaining the authority and independence of courts, or regulating telephony, wireless broadcasting, television, or the exhibition of cinematograph films; or
   - **(b)** imposing restrictions upon persons holding office under the Government of the Federation or a State, members of the Armed Forces, the Nigeria Police Force, or other Government security services or agencies established by law.

---

#### 40. Right to Peaceful Assembly and Association

Every person shall be entitled to assemble freely and associate with other persons, and, in particular, may form or belong to any political party, trade union, or other association for the protection of their interests:

> **Provided that** the provisions of this section shall not derogate from the powers conferred by this Constitution on the Independent National Electoral Commission with respect to political parties to which that Commission does not accord recognition.

---

#### 41. Right to Freedom of Movement

1. Every citizen of Nigeria is entitled to move freely throughout Nigeria and to reside in any part thereof, and no citizen shall be expelled from or refused entry into or exit from Nigeria.

2. Nothing in subsection (1) shall invalidate any law reasonably justifiable in a democratic society:
   - **(a)** imposing restrictions on the residence or movement of any person who has committed or is reasonably suspected to have committed a criminal offence;
   - **(b)** providing for the removal of any person from Nigeria to any other country to:
     - **(i)** be tried outside Nigeria for a criminal offence; or
     - **(ii)** undergo imprisonment outside Nigeria in execution of the sentence of a court for an offence they have been found guilty of.

   > **Provided that** there is a reciprocal agreement between Nigeria and such other country regarding such matters.

---

#### 42. Right to Freedom from Discrimination

1. A citizen of Nigeria shall not, by reason only that they belong to a particular community, ethnic group, place of origin, sex, religion, or political opinion:
   - **(a)** be subjected to disabilities or restrictions to which other citizens of Nigeria are not made subject; or
   - **(b)** be accorded any privilege or advantage not accorded to other citizens.

2. No citizen shall be subjected to any disability or deprivation merely by reason of the circumstances of their birth.

3. Nothing in subsection (1) shall invalidate any law imposing restrictions regarding:
   - Appointments to any office under the State;
   - Membership of the Armed Forces, the Nigeria Police Force;
   - Service in a body corporate established directly by law in Nigeria.

---

#### 43. Right to Acquire and Own Immovable Property Anywhere in Nigeria

Subject to the provisions of this Constitution, every citizen of Nigeria shall have the right to acquire and own immovable property anywhere in Nigeria.

---

#### 44. Compulsory Acquisition of Property

1. No movable property or any interest in immovable property shall be compulsorily acquired except in the manner and for the purposes prescribed by a law that:
   - **(a)** requires prompt payment of compensation; and
   - **(b)** grants the person claiming such compensation a right of access to a court, tribunal, or body having jurisdiction for the determination of their interest and the amount of compensation.

2. Nothing in subsection (1) affects any general law:
   - For the imposition or enforcement of taxes;
   - Imposing penalties or forfeiture for breach of law;
   - Relating to leases, mortgages, contracts, bankruptcies, judgments, or public health;
   - Relating to enemy property, trusts, or limitation of actions;
   - Relating to property of corporate bodies established by law;
   - For temporary possession for investigations;
   - For soil-conservation works;
   - For installation or maintenance of utilities, subject to prompt payment for damages.

3. Notwithstanding these provisions, the entire property and control of all minerals, mineral oils, and natural gas in, under, or upon any land in Nigeria or its territorial waters and Exclusive Economic Zone shall vest in the Government of the Federation.

---

#### 45. Restriction on and Derogation from Fundamental Rights

1. Nothing in sections 37, 38, 39, 40, and 41 shall invalidate any law that is reasonably justifiable in a democratic society:
   - **(a)** in the interest of defence, public safety, public order, public morality, or public health; or
   - **(b)** for protecting the rights and freedom of others.

2. An Act of the National Assembly may, during periods of emergency, derogate from sections 33 or 35 but only to the extent reasonably justifiable to address the emergency:

   > **Provided that** no derogation from section 33 is permitted, except in cases of death resulting from acts of war, and none from section 36(8).

3. A **"period of emergency"** means any period during which a proclamation of emergency by the President under section 305 is in force.

---

#### 46. Special Jurisdiction of High Court and Legal Aid

1. Any person alleging a contravention of the provisions of this Chapter in any State may apply to a High Court in that State for redress.

2. A High Court shall have original jurisdiction to hear and determine such applications and may issue such orders, writs, and directions necessary for enforcing rights within that State.

3. The Chief Justice of Nigeria may make rules governing the practice and procedure of a High Court for this purpose.

4. The National Assembly:
   - **(a)** may confer additional powers on a High Court as necessary to effectively exercise the jurisdiction conferred by this section.
   - **(b)** shall provide:
     - **(i)** financial assistance to indigent citizens whose rights have been infringed or to enable them engage legal counsel; and
     - **(ii)** ensure that such allegations are substantial and that the need for financial or legal aid is genuine.
